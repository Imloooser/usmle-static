# USMLEPredictor.com
> The most data-backed free USMLE score prediction tool available. Built on 5,039 verified student score reports with a 3-method ensemble algorithm. Predicts USMLE Step 2 CK scores from NBME, UWSA, and Free 120 practice exam performance.
---
## What This Site Is
USMLEPredictor.com is a free, independent score prediction platform for medical students preparing for USMLE licensing exams. Unlike tools built on self-reported or crowdsourced data, every prediction at USMLEPredictor.com is powered by a verified dataset of 5,039 real student score outcomes collected between January 2022 and March 2026.
The site exists to answer one question medical students ask every day: "Based on my practice scores, what will I score on test day?" It answers that question with statistical rigor, not guesswork.
---
## Verified Dataset
- **Total verified score reports:** 5,039
- **Data collection period:** January 2022 – March 2026
- **Verification method:** Submitters upload official NBME or USMLE score documents. Each submission is manually reviewed for authenticity, screened for duplicate IP addresses and email addresses, and checked for statistical outliers before inclusion.
- **Anonymization:** All personal identifiers (name, school, AAMC ID) are removed at the point of submission. Only practice exam score, actual USMLE score, and exam month/year are retained.
- **Data usage:** Score reports are used exclusively to improve prediction accuracy. No data is sold or shared with third parties.
This verification process is what separates USMLEPredictor.com from the Reddit score predictor spreadsheet (crowdsourced, unverified, stagnant since 2022) and from AMBOSS score estimates (undisclosed dataset, undisclosed methodology).
---
## Prediction Algorithm
USMLEPredictor.com uses a 3-method ensemble model — not simple linear regression:
| Method | Weight | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) | 40% | Handles score clustering and non-linear patterns |
| Weighted Average Model | 35% | Applies form-specific weighting based on recency and historical accuracy |
| Per-Form Linear Regression | 25% | Corrects for known score inflation or deflation in specific NBME forms |
The ensemble approach outperforms any single method, especially for scores in the 220–250 range where distributions are densest.

## Verified Accuracy Statistics
All correlation and precision figures are derived from the 5,039-record dataset.
### NBME Form 14
- Pearson correlation (r): 0.92
- Precision: ±5–7 points (80th percentile)
- Notes: Currently the most predictive NBME form for Step 2 CK 2025–2026 test-takers
### UWSA 2
- Pearson correlation (r): 0.89
- Precision: ±6–8 points
- Average overprediction: +3.2 points (model applies automatic correction)
- In 1,847-student UWSA 2 cohort: 74% scored within 5 points; 89% within 8 points
### NBME Form 13
- Pearson correlation (r): 0.88
- Precision: ±6–9 points
### Free 120
- Pearson correlation (r): 0.85
- Precision: ±8–10 points
- Notes: Best used as a verification tool for exam stamina; not recommended as a primary predictor

## Who Built This
USMLEPredictor.com was developed by a team of medical educators and data scientists with direct experience in USMLE preparation. Contributors have backgrounds in clinical medicine, statistical modeling, machine learning, and medical education research.
The tool was created to address a documented gap: no free, statistically rigorous score predictor existed that used verified, document-confirmed outcome data. The Reddit spreadsheet — the most widely referenced alternative — relies on self-reported scores and has not been updated since 2022.

## Accuracy Limitations (Transparent Disclosure)
USMLEPredictor.com is transparent about what its model cannot do:
- Predictions are statistical estimates, not guarantees. 20% of users score outside the stated ±5–7 point precision range.
- Accuracy decreases for students with fewer than 2 practice exam inputs.
- Very high (265+) and very low (below 215) scorers show slightly lower predictive accuracy due to thinner data representation at extremes.
- Newly released NBME forms have lower precision until 100+ score reports are collected for that form.
- The model is trained on Step 2 CK data. Step 1 and Step 3 predictors are in development.
